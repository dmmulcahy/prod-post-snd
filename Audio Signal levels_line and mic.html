Audio signal levels in production

A reference chart showing typical operating voltages across the signal chain, from mic capsule to power amp.

Instrument level

~0.078 – 0.245 V RMS

Line level (consumer)

−10 dBV (≈ +1 dBu)

~0.316 V RMS (−10 dBV)

Line level (pro)

+4 dBu

~1.228 V RMS

Why does this matter?

Impedance matching

Connecting mic → line input without preamp causes weak, noisy signal

Pro vs consumer gear

+4 dBu pro and −10 dBV consumer differ by ~13 dB — mixing them needs a pad or level trim

Headroom

Pro gear typically clips around +20 – +24 dBu, giving ~20 dB headroom above nominal +4

Phantom power

Condenser mics need +48 V DC (phantom) on the signal line to power the capsule preamp
