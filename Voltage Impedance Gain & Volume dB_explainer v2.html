Why logarithmic (dB) scale?
The ratio of mic voltage to speaker voltage is about 10,000:1. On a linear scale that's unworkable. Decibels compress it: every ×10 in voltage = +20 dB, every ×2 = +6 dB. The entire audio range fits neatly between −60 dBu and +40 dBu.

Impedance mismatch calculator
Impedance is AC resistance (Ω). When a signal source drives a load, they form a voltage divider — the load's share of the voltage is Z_load / (Z_source + Z_load). The lower the load relative to the source, the more signal is lost.

Rule of thumb: 10:1 ratio
Load should be ≥10× the source impedance for <1 dB loss. Common pairs: mic output (~150–200 Ω) into preamp input (1–10 kΩ) ✓. Guitar pickup (10–50 kΩ) into a standard line input (10 kΩ) ✗ — use a DI box. Mismatches don't just reduce volume; they also roll off high frequencies first, changing the tone.
